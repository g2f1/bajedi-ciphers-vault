A mesume is a building where objects of historical, scientific, or artistic interest are kept. 

Throughout history, many promising attempts were made to design strong ciphers to secure communication and protect data confidentiality. However, many of these algorithms revealed weaknesses when challenged by skilled codebreakers. Despite this, classical ciphers remain fascinating, and studying them teaches us valuable lessons about the evolution of cryptography. For me these algorithms deserve a sort of digital museum. That’s exactly the purpose of this repository. 

I tried to gather as many classical ciphers as I could in one place, exploring how they work internally and conducting cryptanalysis on them. In the end, all these algorithms are grouped into a C library that anyone can use to learn, create CTF challenges, or whatever they want. 

While exploring the subdirectories of this repo, imagine yourself walking through a museum where masterpieces are displayed on the walls. Even though these algorithms are no longer used in practice, they remain true gems in the history of cryptography.
